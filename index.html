🎯 Fixed Festival Dates
Every year, festivals fall on the same weekday:

📅 New Year (Nisan 1): Always Wednesday
Start of the sacred year
🍷 Passover (Nisan 15): Always Wednesday
Exactly 2 weeks after New Year
🌾 First Fruits (Nisan 26): Always Sunday
Day after the Sabbath following Passover
📜 Pentecost (Sivan 15): Always Sunday
Exactly 50 days after First Fruits
🍇 New Wine (Av 3): Always Sunday
50 days after Pentecost
🫒 New Oil (Elul 22): Always Sunday
50 days after New Wine
🕊️ Day of Atonement (Tishrei 10): Always Friday
Day before the Sabbath
🌿 Tabernacles (Tishrei 15): Always Wednesday
Lasts 7 days through Tuesday

📐 Mathematical Precision
Why 364 days is perfect:

• 52 weeks exactly: 364 ÷ 7 = 52 (no remainder!)
• 4 seasons equally: 364 ÷ 4 = 91 days each
• 13 weeks per season: 91 ÷ 7 = 13 weeks
• Every date = same weekday: Perfect alignment forever

🔢 The Formula:
12 months × 30 days = 360 days
+ 4 Tekufah days (seasonal markers)
= 364 days total
📚 Historical Background
Where this calendar comes from:

• Found in the Dead Sea Scrolls at Qumran
• Used by the Zadokite priests
• Mentioned in the Book of Jubilees
• Referenced in Book of Enoch
• Possibly used in First Temple period

Why they used it: They believed the lunar calendar (which changes yearly) disrupted the proper timing of festivals. This solar calendar ensures festivals always align with the same days of the week, maintaining perfect liturgical order.
